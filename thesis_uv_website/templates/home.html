Welcome to this study on chatbot interactions with human users.

To participate in this study, you must be over eighteen (18) years old and fluent in English. The study will take approximately 15 minutes to complete

Purpose of the Study:

This study is part of an undergradate thesis for the Leiden Institute of Advanced Computer Science (LIACS) at Leiden University by CJ Reitter under the supervision of Dr. Peter van der Putten. By participating, you will be contributing to the understanding of human interactions with LLM-based chatbots. The exact interaction being measured will not be disclosed until the end of the study. We ask that you do not disclose the nature of this study after completion to ensure experimental integrity

Procedures:

It is expected that this study will take approximately 15 minutes to complete. You are welcome to stop participating in the study at any time, by simply closing the site.

The study is comprised of 2 phases. In the first, you will be presented with a chatbot powered by Deepseek. It is prompted to communicate with the user as if it were making small talk with a friendly peer or acquantaince, and you are encouraged to communicate with it in a similar fashion. There is no expected minimum or maximum prompt count on the user or agent's end, so you may interact with it at whatever pace you feel comfortable with. You will remain on the page for 5 minutes before you are taken to the second phase of the study.

In the second phase, you will be directed to a form where you will be asked to answer both rating-based and open-ended questions on your perception of the chatbot's behavior and your interactions with it. All open-ended questions are optional and filling them out is not required to complete the study. You will also be asked a few demographic questions (age and sex), which are also optional to disclose.

After submiting, you will be taken to a page disclosing the exact nature of the study and the scales being measured. We ask that you don't share the contents and nature of the experiment for a period of 3 months.

Potential Concerns:

While the exposure length of this chatbot is short and data on "AI-associated psychosis", defined as the increase of delusions and/or paranoia in connection with chatbot interaction, remains anecdotal, we recognize that it is a potential concern when interacting with LLM-based chatbots. We recommend you do not participate if you believe that you may be suceptiable to these symptoms and/or immediately withdraw from the study if communication with the chatbot is causing you distress or discomfort. You always have the right to withdraw from the study for any reason.

We also recognize that LLM-based chatbots can imitate human-like conversation extremely well and have the potential to appear more intimate than they are. We would like everyone participating in the exam to treat their conversation with care and be mindful of what they share with the chatbot, working under the assumption that a stranger could read their chats. Everything the chatbot generates is made up and should not act as a substitute for human-to-human interaction.

Confidentiality:

This research is conducted anonymously, and no identifiable information will be collected. Your data will be used exclusively for this study and will never be shared with third parties by the researcher and supervisor. After the study is completed, the aggregated results may be used for further research or publications. Any and all unpublished raw data collected from the study will be destroyed after 6 months after the termination of the study.

The chatbot runs on the DeepSeek-r1 model on a local server using Ollama. Ourselves and Ollama do not log the contents of the responses or prompts made while communicating with the chatbot, but token and prompt count are recorded by Ollama.

Voluntary Participation

Your participation is completely voluntary. You are under no obligation to complete the survey, and you may withdraw at any time without any penalties. If you choose to exit the survey, you will not be required to provide any justification, and your answers will be deleted. Responses to the questions asked in phase 2 are only retained if they are submitted.
